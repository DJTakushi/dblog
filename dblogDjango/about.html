Favicon Info

Until recently, the character in the favicon used throughout this site is what I‘ve used as the first character for my last name in Japanese:

I recently found out that it’s not actually the correct character. Read on for details.

My family didn’t have any records of the traditional spelling of our family name (that I was aware of). When I was in Japan, I found that I needed a way to write my name in kanji for traditional institutions that can only operate in Japanese scripts (banks, government, etc.).

I knew that there was a district called Takushi in Okinawa, where my family’s name is from:

So I just used that spelling. It wasn’t a bad guess.

According to my preferred online Japanese dictionary, jisho.org, 沢岻 is a legitimate spelling of a family name.

Jisho also indicates that the kanji could mean something like “Mt. Swampy.” It’s not the most flattering translation, but it’s still better than spelling it like a loan-word in katakana (タクシ), which is pretty close to how “taxi” is spelled (タクシー).

Admittedly though, my selected spelling is derived and not obvious. The second character, 岻, was never recognized by anyone that I met in Japan during my time outside of Okinawa. This isn’t terribly surprising since Okinawa has a lot of kanji unique to the Ryukyu Islands, but the use of 沢 is retrospectively questionable.

The kanji 沢 is more commonly pronounced “sawa” in names like Sawada (沢田). But notably, it can also be used to spell takusan (たくさん or 沢山), which means something like “in a notably large quantity” (although spelling for this word is more commonly done in hiragana than kanji):

I kind of like that excessiveness. I appreciate a job well-done.

However, a few days ago, my Dad found a letter from my Grandfather that had been marked with his hanko, using the spelling 澤岻.

The first character is different. Uh-oh.

Jisho affirms that this is also a legitimate spelling:

If 澤岻 is on my Grandfather’s seal, it’s pretty definitively correct.

Unfortunately, I’ve already used 沢岻 for important documents and personal branding. I literally stenciled it on a longboard a few weeks ago. I’m honestly tempted to keep using 沢岻 for convenience and since it’s grown on me. The simpler character also makes for a clearer and bolder favicon.

vs

But, correct should trump convenient. It pains me to rewrite my own name, but as they say back in the Old Country, 仕方がない (shikata ga nai), meaning, “it can’t be helped”. I’ll switch over to the correct spelling for personal branding.

Here’s to re-rediscovering the culture of our ancestors.
